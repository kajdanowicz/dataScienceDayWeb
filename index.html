Wykład Plenarny wygłosi

prof. Jesse Read

Many problems in data science and machine learning involve multiple predictions for each input instance, such in the area of multi-label classification which includes applications as diverse as text categorization, image labeling and segmentation, time series forecasting, localization and tracking, and recommender systems. The family of methods collectively known as 'classifier chains' is one such approach to this type of learning problem.

The main idea is to link together off-the-shelf binary classifiers in a chain structure, such that predictions become additional features for other classifiers. This general idea proved flexible and effective and variations on this method have obtained state-of-the-art performance across many datasets and under several multi-label evaluation metrics. This performance has also raised many questions about how the chaining mechanism works, and how it could be improved. This talk explores some of the studies made of chaining, its perspectives in the modern contexts of multi-label classification, as well as investigating its application in related areas such as those involving continuous output variables.
